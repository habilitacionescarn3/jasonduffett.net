# privacy

This is a personal blog. I'm not selling anything and I don't run ads. The only data
collection here is light-touch Google Analytics, and only if you say yes.

## what is collected

If you accept the cookie banner, Google Analytics 4 records:

- Page views and the order you visit them in
- Where you came from (referrer)
- Approximate location (country / region — derived from your IP, which is then discarded)
- Device class and browser

It does **not** record your name, email, exact IP address, or anything that identifies
you personally. There are no advertising cookies, no cross-site tracking, no
fingerprinting.

If you reject the banner, none of this happens — no cookies are set and no data leaves
your browser. The banner remembers your choice in `localStorage` so you aren't asked
again.

## why

Honestly, just to see which posts land. If something resonates I might write more
of it; if something gets ignored I won't waste my time. That's the whole motivation.

## who processes it

[Google LLC](https://policies.google.com/privacy) processes the data on my behalf as
the GA4 provider. I have IP anonymisation enabled and ad-related signals disabled.
Data is retained for 14 months (GA4 default) and then deleted automatically.

## changing your mind

You can withdraw consent any time using the **cookies** link in the footer of every
page. That re-opens the banner and lets you switch your choice. Rejecting clears the
behaviour going forward; existing GA cookies expire on their own (or you can clear
them in your browser).

## contact

If you have questions or want your data removed, get in touch via the email on my
GitHub profile.
