The Beatles' _Don't Let Me Down_ is a raw, slow-burning Lennon love song with a
chorus you can bawl across a room. The **5/4** bars in the verse leave the listener
hanging for the resolution of the second line, and then in comes that chorus —
"Don't Let Me Down"! I've transposed it down a step to **Dm** for ukulele ergonomics
and included a fair approximation of Billy Preston's Rhodes riff. Big thanks to
UkeHoot's Jonathan for the bass parts.

The Beatles only ever played this one in public once — on the rooftop of the
Apple building in London on 30 January 1969, during what turned out to be their
final live performance. UkeHoot's own debut was a shade lower-key: the 2024
Beatles Marathon at _Portobello Town Hall_ in November 2024, where it went down
a storm. So we're dusting it off for _Routes &amp; Rhythms_ — **UkeHoot**'s 2025
Edinburgh Fringe show.

The transcription below splits the parts so a roomful of ukes can cover the
original between them: melody ukes carry the chords and the four riffs, and a
bass (or a baritone an octave up) takes Macca's bass line — set out separately
for the chorus, verses, middle 8 and outro.

You can hear the original on Spotify:
[Don't Let Me Down — The Beatles](https://open.spotify.com/track/3evG0BIqEFMMP7lVJh1cSf).

To find out more about UkeHoot and upcoming Fringe shows, head to [ukehoot.net](https://ukehoot.net/).
