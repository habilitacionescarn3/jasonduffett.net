Amy Macdonald's _This Is the Life_ is the title track from her 2007 debut — a
Scottish indie-folk anthem built on a single four-chord loop that never lets up.
The whole song circles **Am – F – C – Em**, driven by a chugging "chuck" strum,
a catchy melodic hook, and strings and additional instrumentation (slide guitar?)
adding atmosphere. It was one of our favourite tracks from **UkeHoot**'s 2025
Edinburgh Fringe shows, and it's stayed in the set ever since — we still play it
regularly around toon, and it always gets the crowd singing along.

This arrangement keeps things simple for a roomful of ukes: everyone holds the
Am–F–C–Em loop on a chucking strum leaving room for fiddle or other instruments to
riff over the melody. The tabs below pick out the little instrumental hooks —
_riff1_ is the rolling intro/instrumental run, _riff2_ sits under the second
verse, and _riff3_ answers it in the middle section. Transposed to **Am** from
the original's **C#m**, and it rattles along at about 190 bpm.

You can hear the original on Spotify:
[This Is the Life — Amy Macdonald](https://open.spotify.com/track/2T3jgoAMbBXiMMD8pSc6QL).
And find out more about UkeHoot at [ukehoot.net](https://ukehoot.net/).
