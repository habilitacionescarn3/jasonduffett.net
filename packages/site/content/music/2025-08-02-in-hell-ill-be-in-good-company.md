The Dead South's _In Hell I'll Be in Good Company_ is a banjo-and-cello murder
ballad that has no business being so damn catchy. It lurks in **Gm**, marches
along on an off-beat strum, and is held together by a whistled hook and a banjo
riff that gets everyone stomping along. I saw them perform at _The Usher Hall_
earlier in the year and I knew it was a perfect candidate for a ukulele strum-a-long.

I worked this arrangement for _Routes & Rhythms_ — **UkeHoot**'s 2025 Edinburgh
Fringe show. The transcription below splits the parts out so a roomful of ukes
can cover the original's banjo, cello and vocal lines between them: melody/strumming
ukes carry the chords and whistling, a baritone takes the cello's walking line,
and a banjo-uke handles the rolling banjo riff.

You can hear the original on Spotify:
[In Hell I'll Be in Good Company — The Dead South](https://open.spotify.com/track/6svak3qVN5xdaRDO1K041S).
And find out more about UkeHoot at [ukehoot.net](https://ukehoot.net/).

> _In Hell I'll Be in Good Company_ was written by Colton Crawford, Nate Hilts,
> Danny Kenyon and Scott Pringle, and released by The Dead South on their 2014
> album _Good Company_ (© 2014 Curve Music). The lyrics and the underlying
> composition remain the property of their copyright holders. The arrangement
> below is an unofficial fan transcription, shared for personal study and
> non-commercial performance — no claim of ownership is made over the song
> itself.
